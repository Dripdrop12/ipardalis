Flash

on January 10, 2015

Flash was our first generation breeder from Cowboy (CH) at Chameleon’s Paradise. He had a very gentle personality and only gave us one healthy clutch with Judy, from which we kept Jude. We were still learning when we got Flash and he passed after consuming a large number of wild bugs when pesticides had been applied to the lawn behind ours. The vet said that he had kidney failure like he’d been poisoned, but we have been continuing to improve our husbandry year-after-year in his honor.

Pituophis deppei jani

on January 1, 0001

We have a small breeding group of ‘18 and ‘19 Pituophis deppei jani (Northern Mexican Pine Snakes). They are a beautiful big-bodied Pituophis species that start out “ugly,” brown and tan. They are highly variable, and as they age, their colors become more distinct. Many animals develop an orange head and tail. Their back saddle markings wash out in the middle and are crisp near the extremities. They can grow to be up to 9’ long and are thicker than most colubrids.

About us

Individually-raised Panther Chameleons. We're a chameleon breeder located in Poolesville, Maryland. We are working with our 7th generation of Ambilobe and 2nd gen of Furcifer minor (Lesser Chameleons). We also have a few colubrid and ball python projects. As lifelong reptile keepers, we hold memberships in Responsible Reptile Keeping and USARK and we sponsor Reptifiles and the Chameleon Forums.
